My History with BenchPrep

After Groupon raised a billion dollars and Lightbank formed, Groupons founders wanted to invest in something educational. They wrote their first check to BenchPrep and founders Ashish and Ujjwal brought me on board as the first employee to help build the business. Back then, it was called, Watermelon Express.

The two founders had already started something great, but it wasn't going to scale, and very little money was actually being made. I went to town; we re-branded, re-designed and re-inveted the product, a few times. Ultimately, the time came when our board wanted us to really start making some money; afterall, we'd built an amazing product, some say it was even over-engineered.

My Contribution

I wore many hats in the beginning, ranging from Art and User Experience director to Head of Marketing. Ultimately, I was responsible for helping the company get to the million dollar run rate. It was a team effort, but I was responsible, and it's why I ended up owning a small piece of the business. While I musn't disclose exactly how we created our black box, I can tell you a few things. At a certain point, 10,0000 new emails were aggregated a day through our mouse traps, and of those emails, we figured out the right formula for converting a healthy enough percentage to raise more 10 million dollars in funding from New Enterprise Associates. After rasiing the funds, vesting and growing to more than 30 employees, I moved on and started a second business with Eric Lefkofsky and Brad Keywell.
